    Looking ahead to the next 20 years, the Gates Foundation has three major aspirations to guide our work: ​
    ​
    1. No mom, child, or baby dies of a preventable cause. ​
    2. The next generation grows up in a world without deadly infectious diseases. ​
    3. Hundreds of millions of people break free from poverty, putting more countries on a path to prosperity. ​
     ​
    I know that with the right commitment of resources, a fairer and healthier future for everyone is just on the horizon.

    It's hard to overstate how amazing this news is. When left untreated, sleeping sickness is a death sentence—but for years, the cure was nearly as dangerous as the disease itself: an arsenic-based drug that killed 1 in 20 patients.​
    ​
    That changed in 2018 with fexinidazole, an incredible, safe, 10-day treatment. And now, sleeping sickness can be cured with a single dose of three simple pills—acoziborole—no matter how advanced it is. What once required dangerous or lengthy care can now be treated easily and safely. Thanks to     Sanofi's commitment to donate the medicine, it will reach patients free of charge. I'm incredibly proud that the Gates Foundation was able to support this initiative.​

    Drugs for Neglected Diseases initiative (DNDi)

    1w • Edited •

    🚀 Huge news for people living with sleeping sickness!

    Acoziborole, a one-day, single-dose oral treatment, has received a positive opinion from the     European Medicines Agency (EMA) for the treatment of the most common form of sleeping sickness, T.b. gambiense.

    💊 DNDi’s Dr Wilfried remembers treating patients with the old medicine which was so toxic it killed one in twenty patients. Developed together with Sanofi, acoziborole is a revolution since those dark days. It cures sleeping sickness with one-day treatment course of three pills and is effective for both stages of the disease, including severe cases.

    Thanks to its safety profile, it can be provided at the village level. Acoziborole will therefore become a major tool to facilitate efforts to finally eliminate sleeping sickness.

    🤝 This achievement reflects over a decade of collaboration and innovation across the global health community. We thank our partners, donors, staff, and everyone who was involved in making the dream of a single-dose, oral treatment for sleeping sickness come true for the people affected.

    📽️ Over the next weeks, we will be sharing stories of some of  those who played a role in making medical history, and what this milestone means. Follow us to hear their voices!

            m-mama solves one of global health’s most persistent problems: getting pregnant women in crisis to the hospital in time. Since 2013, m-mama's community drivers have responded to more than 125,000 emergencies and saved an estimated 5,266 lives. The program—a sort of 911 for expectant mothers in rural Africa—is a great reminder that not all breakthroughs in global health come in the form of new drugs or sophisticated technology. Sometimes, they’re just better ways of getting the right care to the right person at the right time.
